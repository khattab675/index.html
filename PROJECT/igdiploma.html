Admission Requirements for International Students

Welcome to [University Name]! We are excited to welcome students from around the world to our campus. Below you will find the requirements for international (IG) students applying for undergraduate programs. Make sure to review the information carefully before starting your application process.

General Admission Requirements

  • Secondary Education: Completion of secondary education (high school diploma or equivalent) recognized by the university.
  • Minimum GPA: A minimum GPA of 3.0 or equivalent on a 4.0 scale (or the equivalent grading system of your home country).
  • English Proficiency: International students must demonstrate proficiency in English through one of the following methods:
    • TOEFL: Minimum score of 80 (iBT) or 550 (PBT).
    • IELTS: Minimum score of 6.0 to 6.5 (depending on the program).
    • Cambridge English Exams: Minimum C1 Advanced level (CAE) or Proficiency level (CPE).
    • English-Medium Education: If you have completed secondary school in an English-speaking institution, you may be exempt from this requirement.

Program-Specific Requirements

Each academic program may have additional specific requirements. Below are some general guidelines:

  • STEM Programs: Applicants should have completed relevant coursework in subjects like mathematics, science, and technology. A grade of B or higher in these subjects is preferred.
  • Business & Economics: Strong background in mathematics and economics is recommended. Some programs may also require standardized test scores (e.g., SAT, ACT).
  • Creative Arts and Humanities: Portfolios, writing samples, or other creative submissions may be required for certain programs (e.g., Fine Arts, Architecture, Journalism).

Application Documents

To apply, you will need to submit the following documents:

  • Completed Application Form: Submit your online application form through our admissions portal.
  • Official Transcripts: Provide your high school transcripts, along with a certified English translation (if applicable).
  • Proof of English Proficiency: Submit your TOEFL, IELTS, or other accepted test scores, or proof of English-medium education.
  • Recommendation Letters: Two or more letters of recommendation from teachers or other academic professionals.
  • Passport Copy: A copy of your valid passport for identification purposes.
  • Financial Documents: Proof of financial support to cover tuition and living expenses during your studies (e.g., bank statements, affidavit of support).

Important Notes:

  • Application Deadline: Please ensure you submit your application at least 6 months prior to the start of the academic year.
  • Visa Requirements: All international students will need to obtain a student visa for study in [Country]. Our admissions team will assist with this process once your application is accepted.
