Admission Requirements for Canadian Students

Welcome to [University Name]! We are excited to welcome students from across Canada to our campus. Below you will find the admission requirements for Canadian students applying for undergraduate programs. Please review the information carefully before starting your application process.

General Admission Requirements

  • Secondary Education: Completion of a Canadian high school diploma (e.g., Ontario Secondary School Diploma, Quebec's Diplôme d'études collégiales, or an equivalent recognized high school qualification from another province or territory).
  • Minimum GPA: A minimum overall average of 75% or equivalent in your high school courses. Please refer to the specific program requirements for the exact GPA needed, as some programs may have higher requirements.
  • English Proficiency: If you completed your high school education in English in Canada, you do not need to demonstrate additional English proficiency. However, if you completed your high school education in French or another language, you may need to submit one of the following English proficiency test scores:
    • TOEFL: Minimum score of 80 (iBT) or 550 (PBT).
    • IELTS: Minimum score of 6.0 to 6.5 (depending on the program).
    • Cambridge English Exams: Minimum C1 Advanced level (CAE) or Proficiency level (CPE).

Program-Specific Requirements

In addition to the general admission requirements, each academic program may have specific prerequisites. Below are some general guidelines for Canadian students:

  • STEM Programs: A strong background in mathematics and science is required. Students should have completed relevant high school courses such as Calculus, Algebra, Chemistry, and Physics. A grade of B or higher in these subjects is preferred.
  • Business & Economics: A background in Mathematics is recommended, and some programs may require advanced math courses (e.g., Calculus or Advanced Functions). Some programs may also require standardized test scores (e.g., SAT, ACT).
  • Creative Arts and Humanities: For certain programs such as Fine Arts, Architecture, or Journalism, you may be required to submit a portfolio, writing sample, or other creative submissions.

Application Documents

To apply, you will need to submit the following documents:

  • Completed Application Form: Submit your online application form through our admissions portal.
  • Official High School Transcripts: Provide your official high school transcripts, along with a certified English or French translation (if applicable), from your high school.
  • Standardized Test Scores: Submit your SAT or ACT scores if required by your program. Some programs may be test-optional, so please check the specific program requirements.
  • Proof of English Proficiency: If applicable, submit your TOEFL, IELTS, or other accepted test scores to demonstrate English proficiency.
  • Recommendation Letters: Two or more letters of recommendation from teachers or other academic professionals who are familiar with your academic performance.
  • Personal Statement or Essays: Many programs require a personal statement or essay as part of your application. This is an opportunity to tell us about yourself, your interests, and why you wish to study at [University Name].
  • Passport Copy: A copy of your valid passport for identification purposes. (For non-Canadian citizens or permanent residents.)
  • Financial Documents: Proof of financial support to cover tuition and living expenses may be required for certain students (e.g., bank statements, affidavit of support).

Important Notes:

  • Application Deadline: Please ensure you submit your application at least 6 months prior to the start of the academic year. Early application deadlines may apply for certain programs.
  • Health Insurance: All students are required to have health insurance while studying at [University Name]. You may opt for the university’s health insurance plan or provide proof of your own coverage.
  • Campus Visits: We encourage prospective students to visit our campus, attend an admissions information session, or take part in virtual tours to learn more about our programs and campus life.
