Introduction to Engineering

The Engineering Major offers students a deep understanding of the principles of engineering, mathematics, science, and technology. Students learn how to apply scientific principles to design, build, and improve systems, structures, and machines.

Engineering is a broad field with various specializations, including Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Chemical, and Software Engineering. It plays a crucial role in innovation and technological advancement in society.

Course Structure

Here are some common courses you may encounter in an Engineering program:

  • Mathematics for Engineers
  • Physics for Engineers
  • Engineering Mechanics
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Electrical Circuits and Systems
  • Materials Science
  • Thermodynamics
  • Control Systems
  • Software Engineering (for Computer Engineers)

Skills Learned in Engineering

Throughout your Engineering studies, you will develop valuable skills such as:

  • Problem-solving and critical thinking
  • Strong analytical abilities
  • Hands-on technical skills
  • Teamwork and collaboration
  • Project management and leadership
  • Mathematical and computational proficiency
  • Attention to detail and precision

Career Opportunities

Graduating with an Engineering degree opens up a wide range of career opportunities in various industries, including:

  • Civil Engineering: Designing infrastructure, roads, bridges, and buildings.
  • Mechanical Engineering: Working on machines, engines, and energy systems.
  • Electrical Engineering: Designing electrical systems, power plants, and electronics.
  • Software Engineering: Developing software applications, systems, and tools.
  • Environmental Engineering: Addressing environmental challenges and sustainability issues.
  • Biomedical Engineering: Designing medical devices and healthcare technology.
