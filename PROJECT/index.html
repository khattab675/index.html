About Cairo University

Cairo University, also known as "Fakultet al-QƒÅhira," was founded in 1908 and is Egypt's oldest and largest university. It has become one of the leading academic institutions in the Arab world and is renowned for its contributions to education, research, and the development of highly skilled professionals across various fields.

Our Mission

The mission of Cairo University is to provide high-quality education, foster innovative research, and contribute to the community by offering programs that align with the changing demands of society. The university strives to shape the future of students through academic excellence and cultural diversity.

Academic Excellence

With over 27 faculties and institutes, Cairo University offers a wide range of undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral programs in various disciplines, including engineering, medicine, social sciences, humanities, and natural sciences. The university is committed to providing an enriching learning environment, where students can access state-of-the-art facilities and collaborate with distinguished professors.

Research and Innovation

Research is at the heart of Cairo University's mission. We promote innovative research in a wide array of fields, from scientific advancements to social studies. The university encourages students and faculty members to actively participate in research that addresses both local and global challenges.

Campus and Facilities

The Cairo University campus, located in the heart of Giza, is equipped with modern academic buildings, libraries, research centers, sports facilities, and student lounges. The campus provides a welcoming and supportive atmosphere for students, offering everything they need to succeed academically and socially.

International Collaboration

Cairo University actively collaborates with universities and research institutions across the globe. These partnerships provide students and faculty with opportunities for international exchange, collaborative research, and exposure to global academic practices.
