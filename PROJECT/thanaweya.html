Admission Requirements for Thanaweya Amma Students

Welcome to [University Name]! We are excited to welcome students from Egypt to our campus. Below you will find the admission requirements for students holding the Thanaweya Amma (Egyptian Secondary School Certificate) applying for undergraduate programs. Please review the information carefully before starting your application process.

General Admission Requirements

  • Secondary Education: Completion of the Thanaweya Amma (Egyptian Secondary School Certificate) or an equivalent secondary school qualification recognized by the university.
  • Minimum GPA: A minimum overall score of 70% or equivalent in your Thanaweya Amma or other recognized secondary school qualifications. Some programs may require a higher GPA, especially competitive programs like Medicine or Engineering, so please check the specific program requirements.
  • English Proficiency: Since the Thanaweya Amma is primarily taught in Arabic, you may need to demonstrate proficiency in English through one of the following methods, unless your program of study is offered in Arabic:
    • TOEFL: Minimum score of 80 (iBT) or 550 (PBT).
    • IELTS: Minimum score of 6.0 to 6.5 (depending on the program).
    • Cambridge English Exams: Minimum C1 Advanced level (CAE) or Proficiency level (CPE).
    • English-Medium Education: If you have completed secondary education in an English-speaking institution or a program where English was the primary language of instruction, you may be exempt from this requirement.

Program-Specific Requirements

Each academic program may have specific requirements. Below are some general guidelines for Thanaweya Amma students:

  • STEM Programs: A strong foundation in Mathematics and Science is required. Applicants should have completed relevant high school courses such as Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry. A grade of 80% or higher in these subjects is preferred.
  • Business & Economics: A background in Mathematics and Economics is recommended. Some programs may also require standardized test scores (e.g., SAT, ACT) or additional coursework in economics or accounting.
  • Creative Arts and Humanities: For certain programs such as Fine Arts, Architecture, or Journalism, portfolios, writing samples, or other creative submissions may be required.

Application Documents

To apply, you will need to submit the following documents:

  • Completed Application Form: Submit your online application form through our admissions portal.
  • Official Thanaweya Amma Transcripts: Provide your official Thanaweya Amma transcript, along with a certified English or Arabic translation (if applicable).
  • Proof of English Proficiency: Submit your TOEFL, IELTS, or other accepted test scores, or proof of English-medium education if applicable.
  • Standardized Test Scores: Submit your SAT or ACT scores if required by your program. Some programs may be test-optional, so please check the program’s specific requirements.
  • Recommendation Letters: Two or more letters of recommendation from teachers or academic professionals who are familiar with your academic performance.
  • Personal Statement or Essays: Many programs require a personal statement or essay as part of your application. This is your opportunity to tell us about yourself, your academic interests, and why you wish to study at [University Name].
  • Passport Copy: A copy of your valid passport for identification purposes (if you are not an Egyptian national or a permanent resident of Egypt).
  • Financial Documents: Proof of financial support to cover tuition and living expenses during your studies (e.g., bank statements, affidavit of support). This may be required for non-Egyptian applicants or in cases where tuition fees apply.

Important Notes:

  • Application Deadline: Please ensure you submit your application at least 6 months prior to the start of the academic year. Some programs may have earlier deadlines, so check specific program requirements.
  • Visa Requirements: If you are a non-Egyptian national, you may need to obtain a student visa to study in [Country]. Our admissions team will assist you with this process once your application is accepted.
  • Health Insurance: All international students are required to have health insurance while studying at [University Name]. You may opt for the university’s health insurance plan or provide proof of your own coverage.
  • Campus Visits: We encourage prospective students from Egypt to visit our campus and attend virtual or in-person information sessions to learn more about our programs and campus life.
