About Chemical Engineering

Chemical Engineering is the branch of engineering that deals with the design, operation, and optimization of processes that transform raw materials into valuable products. This field integrates principles of chemistry, physics, mathematics, biology, and economics to develop sustainable solutions in industries like energy, pharmaceuticals, food production, and environmental protection.

Core Subjects in Chemical Engineering

  • Thermodynamics and Heat Transfer
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Chemical Reaction Engineering
  • Transport Phenomena
  • Process Control and Optimization
  • Materials Science and Engineering
  • Biochemical Engineering
  • Environmental Engineering
  • Safety and Risk Management
  • Process Design and Simulation

Career Opportunities

Chemical Engineering graduates have the opportunity to work in various industries, such as pharmaceuticals, energy, petrochemicals, and manufacturing. Some of the common career paths include:

  • Process Engineer
  • Chemical Plant Manager
  • Environmental Engineer
  • Pharmaceutical Engineer
  • Materials Engineer
  • Biochemical Engineer
  • Safety and Risk Engineer
  • Energy Systems Engineer
  • Product Development Specialist
  • Research and Development (R&D) Engineer
